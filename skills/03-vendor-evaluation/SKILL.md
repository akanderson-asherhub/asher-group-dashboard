# Vendor Evaluation Toolkit Skill

## Engagement Context

**Positioning:** Structured vendor selection process that reduces buyer risk, drives competitive tension, and ensures best-fit tool adoption. Leads to better outcomes, higher ROI realization, and smoother implementations.

**Pricing:** $5,000–$15,000 (depends on number of vendors evaluated and technical depth required)
**Duration:** 4–8 weeks (varies by number of finalists; reference check calls add 2–3 weeks)
**Reusability:** ~65% of deliverables (RFP template, scorecard framework, TCO model, reference guide) are reusable across clients; 35% customized to client's specific requirements, vendor list, and negotiation context
**Client archetypes:** GC/ENR 400, Owner/REIT, PE/CVC (due diligence), mid-market specialty contractors
**Conversion potential:** Often leads to implementation support engagement ($25K–$75K implementation advisory) or post-contract vendor management ($5K–$8K/month, 2–3 days)

## Four-Stage Evaluation Process

### Stage 1: Requirements Definition (Week 1–2)

**Objective:** Document what the client actually needs, not what they think they want.

**Inputs (from discovery conversation with client leadership):**

1. **Operational requirements:** What specific workflows/functions must the tool support?
   - Example for PM software: "Multi-project controls, real-time crew productivity tracking, field-to-office data sync, integration with Procore and Autodesk Build, mobile app for foremen, reporting dashboard"

2. **User roles:** Who will use this tool daily? Weekly? Occasionally?
   - Example: Project Managers (daily), PMs (daily), Superintendents (daily), Finance (weekly), Executives (monthly)

3. **Data integrations:** What other systems must this tool integrate with?
   - Current tech stack: Procore, QuickBooks, Excel, Slack, Microsoft Teams, Dropbox, etc.
   - Must-haves vs. nice-to-haves

4. **Scalability:** Deployment scope?
   - Pilot: 1 project, 50 users
   - Full deployment: 20 projects, 500 users
   - Future growth: 1,000+ users in 3 years?

5. **Technical constraints:** Infrastructure, security, compliance, data residency?
   - Cloud vs. on-premise requirements
   - GDPR, HIPAA, SOC 2 compliance mandates
   - Data location requirements (EU data center? US only?)
   - API and integration requirements

6. **Timeline:** When must the tool be operational?
   - Pilot start: Q3 2026?
   - Full deployment: Q4 2026?
   - Hard deadline or flexible?

7. **Budget:** What is the client willing to invest over 3 and 5 years?
   - Licensing
   - Implementation
   - Training
   - Support / managed services
   - Total budget ceiling?

8. **Organizational readiness:** Change management capacity?
   - Has organization successfully adopted tools in the past?
   - Training/learning capacity?
   - Resistance risk factors?

**Deliverable:** Requirements Matrix (DOCX or XLSX)
- 2–3 page summary of above; used as RFP brief and scorecard validation baseline

---

### Stage 2: Long-List Identification (Week 2–3)

**Objective:** Identify 10–15 vendors that could plausibly meet requirements. Use landscape map (if available) + research.

**Process:**

1. **Reference landscape map** (if this engagement follows Tech Landscape Map subscription)
   - Filter by lifecycle phase, technology category, maturity stage
   - Start with Mature and Growth vendors; include 1–2 Emerging if client wants innovation

2. **Add vendors from industry reports** (BuiltWorlds Building Tech 50, CREtech, ENR annual rankings)

3. **Add market knowledge** (from Aaron's consulting experience, peer recommendations)

4. **Tier vendors by likelihood:**
   - **Tier 1 (Likely fit):** Vendors with strong category reputation, references in client's industry segment, clear feature alignment = 5–7 vendors
   - **Tier 2 (Possible fit):** Newer vendors with interesting feature set but less track record, or horizontal vendors with construction module = 3–5 vendors
   - **Tier 3 (Reach):** Long-shot vendors with 1–2 unique features but unclear implementation experience = 1–2 vendors

**Deliverable:** Vendor Shortlist Document (DOCX)
- Vendor name, category, maturity stage, 2–3 sentence description, rationale for inclusion, tier
- Total: 10–15 vendors

### Stage 3: Weighted Scorecard & Short-List (Week 3–6)

**Objective:** Objectively evaluate vendors against criteria, identify 3–5 finalists, prepare for reference checks and negotiation.

#### 3A: RFP Issuance

**Deliverable: DOCX RFP Template**

**Section 1: Cover Letter**
- Context: Client's business, project/initiative, evaluation timeline, expected response deadline
- Example: "We are a $250M commercial GC evaluating Project Management software for 15 active projects across 3 regions. We seek a partner for 3-year engagement with potential 5-year extension."

**Section 2: Company/Vendor Profile Questions**
- Years in business, revenue, customer count, funding, key investors, executive team
- GDPR/HIPAA/SOC 2 certifications
- Data center locations, disaster recovery, SLA uptime guarantee
- Customer concentration: % of revenue from top 5 customers, customer retention rate
- References: 3–5 customer references in AEC industry

**Section 3: Requirements Matrix**
- Structured evaluation form: "Does your solution support: [Requirement #1]?"
  - Response options: Yes / Partial / No / Not Applicable
  - For each, provide brief explanation and link to documentation/feature set
- Requirements organized by category (Operational, Data Integration, Scalability, Security, Support, Pricing)
- ~30–50 requirements total

**Section 4: Implementation & Support**
- Implementation approach: How long to full deployment? Methodology? Change management included?
- Training: How many hours included? Train-the-trainer model? Ongoing training available?
- Support: SLA response times? Escalation path? Dedicated customer success manager?
- Integration: How are integrations with [Procore, QuickBooks, etc.] handled? Cost and timeline?
- Customization: What's included in base license? What's custom? Cost model for customization?
- Data migration: How is data from current system migrated? Timeline? Risk mitigation?

**Section 5: Pricing & Commercial**
- License cost: Per user? Per project? Flat platform fee? Volume discounts?
- Multi-year commitment: 1-year, 3-year, 5-year options? Discount for longer commitment?
- Implementation cost: Fixed or time-and-materials? What's included vs. extra?
- Training cost: Hours included in implementation? Additional training cost?
- Support cost: Included in license or separate? tiered support options?
- Other costs: Integration, customization, professional services, data migration

**Section 6: Vendor Positioning**
- How do you position your solution against [Competitor 1], [Competitor 2], [Competitor 3]?
- Why should we choose you? Unique differentiators?
- 3–5 year roadmap: What's coming? How do we influence roadmap?
- Pricing trends: Will pricing increase? How much? Annual CPI adjustment or step increases?

**Section 7: Response Instructions**
- Response format: DOCX or PDF, max 20 pages
- Response deadline: [Date, 2 weeks out]
- Submission: Email to [contact], subject line "[Vendor Name] RFP Response – [Client Name]"
- Evaluation timeline: We will evaluate responses and contact shortlist vendors by [Date]. Final selection by [Date].

#### 3B: Weighted Scorecard Framework (XLSX)

**Scoring structure:**
- Criteria (rows) × Vendors (columns)
- Each criteria row has: Weighting %, Description, Scoring Scale (1–5), Raw Score, Weighted Score
- Bottom row sums all weighted scores for each vendor

**Criteria & Benchmark Weights (From Asher Group Benchmarks — DO NOT MODIFY):**

| Criterion | Weight | Definition |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Functional Fit** | 25% | Does the solution address core requirements? Feature completeness, workflow alignment, user experience, mobile capability, reporting depth |
| **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)** | 20% | All-in cost: licensing + implementation + training + support + integration over 3-year and 5-year periods. ROI payback period. |
| **Technical Architecture** | 15% | Platform scalability, cloud infrastructure, uptime/SLA, security (SOC 2, encryption, 2FA), API richness, integration ease, custom development capability |
| **Industry Expertise** | 15% | Track record in construction/AEC. Customer references in similar firm size/project type. Domain knowledge in team. Construction-specific workflows vs. generic platform |
| **Support & Service** | 15% | Implementation support quality (dedicated PM, change management). SLA response times (critical, urgent, standard tiers). Escalation path. Customer success program maturity. Training depth. |
| **Innovation & Roadmap** | 10% | Product roadmap aligned with industry trends (BIM, AI, mobile, integrations). Vendor investment in R&D. Frequency of feature releases. Response to customer requests. |

**Scoring Scale (1–5):**
- 1 = Does not meet requirement / Poor performance / Major gaps
- 2 = Partially meets requirement / Below expectation / Significant gaps
- 3 = Meets requirement / Acceptable / Minor gaps
- 4 = Exceeds requirement / Strong performance / Minimal gaps
- 5 = Significantly exceeds requirement / Excellent performance / No gaps

**Calculation:**
- Raw Score × Weight% = Weighted Score (for each criterion, each vendor)
- Sum weighted scores across all criteria for total vendor score (0–5 scale)

**Example:**
| Criterion | Weight | Vendor A | Vendor B | Vendor C |
|-----------|--------|----------|----------|----------|
| Functional Fit (25%) | 25% | 4 × 0.25 = 1.0 | 5 × 0.25 = 1.25 | 3 × 0.25 = 0.75 |
| TCO (20%) | 20% | 3 × 0.20 = 0.6 | 4 × 0.20 = 0.8 | 4 × 0.20 = 0.8 |
| Tech Architecture (15%) | 15% | 4 × 0.15 = 0.6 | 4 × 0.15 = 0.6 | 3 × 0.15 = 0.45 |
| Industry Expertise (15%) | 15% | 3 × 0.15 = 0.45 | 4 × 0.15 = 0.6 | 5 × 0.15 = 0.75 |
| Support (15%) | 15% | 3 × 0.15 = 0.45 | 4 × 0.15 = 0.6 | 3 × 0.15 = 0.45 |
| Innovation (10%) | 10% | 3 × 0.10 = 0.3 | 4 × 0.10 = 0.4 | 2 × 0.10 = 0.2 |
| **TOTAL SCORE** | 100% | **3.4** | **4.25** | **3.4** |

**Interpretation:**
- Vendor B (4.25) = Clear winner, short list for reference checks and negotiation
- Vendors A & C (tied at 3.4) = Review and discuss trade-offs; consider depth scoring for one to break tie, or both to short list

#### 3C: Short-List Selection Criteria

**Process:**
1. Score all 10–15 vendors using RFP responses
2. Rank by total score
3. Select top 3–5 for reference checks and deeper evaluation

**Selection rules:**
- **If clear separation:** Top 3 vendors with scores >4.0 = short list
- **If close competition:** Top 5 vendors with scores >3.5 = short list, but be prepared to narrow to 3 before reference checks
- **If tiers evident:** Maybe Tier 1 vendors (4.0+) go to short list; Tier 2 vendors (3.0–3.9) held as backups
- **Recommendation:** Always include at least one "incumbent competitor" (e.g., if client uses Procore today, include Procore in short list as reference point for improvement)

**Deliverable: Short-List Recommendation Memo (DOCX)**
- Executive summary: "Based on RFP scoring, we recommend evaluating these 3 finalists: [Vendor 1] (4.3), [Vendor 2] (4.1), [Vendor 3] (3.9)"
- Rationale for each: Key strengths and trade-offs
- What we need from reference checks to finalize decision
- Timeline for next steps (reference checks, pilot proposal, final recommendation)

### Stage 4: Reference Checks & Negotiation (Week 5–8)

#### 4A: Reference Check Interview Guide (DOCX)

**Deliverable: Reference Check Interview Template**

**Purpose:** Validate RFP claims with actual customer experience; uncover implementation risks and ROI realization; build confidence in vendor capability.

**Pre-Call Preparation:**
- Email client reference 1 week in advance: "We are evaluating [Vendor] as part of our software selection. Could you spare 30 minutes for a reference call? Here are topics we'd like to discuss: implementation experience, support quality, feature adoption, ROI realization, and what you'd do differently."
- Research reference customer: firm size, project types, implementation date, known case studies
- Identify if reference is current user or former (how recent is their experience?)

**Reference Call Script (8–10 questions, 30–45 minutes):**

1. **Implementation Experience (5 min)**
   - How long was your implementation? Was it on-time and on-budget?
   - What was the biggest challenge during implementation?
   - How did the vendor handle customization requests? Were they delivered?
   - If you had to do it again, what would you do differently?

2. **Feature Adoption & Usability (5 min)**
   - Which features have been most valuable to your firm?
   - Which features have NOT been adopted? Why?
   - How long did it take your team to become productive on the platform?
   - Mobile app: Is it actually used on job sites, or do people still prefer desktop/paper?

3. **Support Quality & Vendor Responsiveness (5 min)**
   - How responsive is the vendor's support team? Typical response time for issues?
   - Have you had escalation situations? How were they handled?
   - Does the vendor have a dedicated customer success manager? How helpful?
   - Training: Did the vendor's training match your team's learning pace? Any gaps?

4. **ROI Realization (5 min)**
   - What metrics did you expect to improve? (labor efficiency, schedule, quality, cost control, etc.)
   - What metrics have actually improved? By how much?
   - Payback period: How long to recoup implementation + licensing costs?
   - If you hadn't implemented this tool, what would you estimate you'd lose competitively?

5. **Integration & Data Quality (5 min)**
   - How well does the tool integrate with your other systems? (Procore, QuickBooks, ERP, etc.)
   - Data quality: Is data coming through correctly, or are there sync issues?
   - Reporting: Are the standard reports useful, or do you need custom reports / exports?

6. **Vendor Stability & Roadmap (5 min)**
   - Do you feel confident the vendor will be around in 5 years?
   - Are they investing in innovations relevant to your business? (AI, mobile, integrations?)
   - Have there been major pricing increases? How frequently?
   - What features would you like to see added in the next 12–24 months?

7. **Competitive Context (5 min)**
   - Are you aware of [Competitor 1]? If so, how does [Vendor] compare?
   - Would you recommend [Vendor] to a peer firm? Any caveats?

8. **Risk & Hidden Costs (5 min)**
   - Were there any hidden costs you didn't anticipate? (integration, customization, data migration, ongoing support?)
   - Any regulatory, security, or compliance surprises?
   - If you could change one thing about the vendor, what would it be?

9. **Benchmark Questions (If time allows)**
   - How many users have you deployed to?
   - Typical user adoption rate in your firm? (% of eligible users who actively use the tool)
   - How often do you upgrade / use new features after the initial implementation?

**Note-Taking & Follow-Up:**
- Take detailed notes on each answer
- After call, send thank-you email with summary of key points for verification
- Flag any contradictions with RFP claims (if vendor said "2-week implementation" but reference says "6 months," that's critical)
- Assign confidence score: High / Medium / Low confidence in vendor's claims

**Aggregate reference insights:**
- Create 1-page summary across all reference calls for each vendor
- Highlight patterns (e.g., "All 3 references noted support response times are slower than promised SLA")

#### 4B: Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Model (XLSX)

**Purpose:** Quantify all-in cost and compare total economic value across vendors (not just license price).

**Deliverable: TCO Calculator (XLSX)**

**Cost Categories:**

1. **Software Licensing (Year 1–3 and Year 4–5)**
   - Per-user annual cost × number of users
   - Platform/flat fee (if applicable)
   - Year 1 discount or ramp-up (often discounted)
   - Year 2–3 list price
   - Year 4–5 projection (assume 3–5% annual increase or step increases per contract)
   - Multi-year discount (if 3-year commitment cheaper than 1-year × 3)

2. **Implementation Costs**
   - Professional services (days × daily rate, or fixed fee)
   - Data migration (hourly effort or fixed)
   - Process documentation and workflow design
   - Custom integration development (if needed)
   - One-time setup and configuration

3. **Training Costs**
   - Included in implementation? Or separate?
   - Instructor-led training (per participant × number of sessions)
   - Train-the-trainer program (if applicable)
   - Online training platform license (if vendor has separate e-learning costs)
   - Ongoing training (annual refresher, new hire onboarding)

4. **Internal Costs (Client Responsibility, but included in TCO)**
   - Project manager / owner resource (% FTE × 3 months for go-live)
   - Business analyst for requirements and customization (% FTE × 6 months)
   - IT / infrastructure support (one-time setup + ongoing)
   - Change management and communications effort

5. **Integration & APIs**
   - Vendor professional services for integrations (if not included in implementation)
   - Third-party integration platform costs (Zapier, MuleSoft, etc., if needed)
   - Custom API development (if standard integrations insufficient)

6. **Support & Maintenance**
   - Annual support contract cost (often 15–20% of license cost)
   - Premium support (additional hours, SLA guarantees)
   - Bug fix and patch installation
   - System administration and helpdesk staff (internal cost)

7. **Infrastructure (Cloud or On-Premise)**
   - Cloud hosting costs (if not included in license)
   - On-premise hardware, servers, backup systems
   - Network upgrades to support tool
   - Cybersecurity infrastructure (firewalls, SSO, 2FA, VPN)

8. **Contingency**
   - 10–15% buffer for unexpected costs
   - Cost overrun risk (implementations often exceed budget by 10–20%)

**Calculation Structure (XLSX Example):**

| Implementation | $30K | — | — | — | — | $30K | $30K |
| Training | $10K | $5K | $5K | $5K | $5K | $20K | $30K |

#### 4C: Vendor Negotiation Strategy

**Post-reference-check, pre-contract:**

**Negotiation levers:**

1. **Pricing:** Volume discount (additional users beyond pilot), multi-year commitment discount, implementation discount
   - E.g., "If we commit to 3-year license, can you reduce Year 1 licensing by 15%?"

2. **Implementation:** Vendor absorbs integration costs, faster timeline (prioritized resources), additional free training
   - E.g., "Can you absorb $10K of integration costs given our multi-year commitment?"

3. **Support:** Dedicated customer success manager, premium SLA (faster response times), enhanced training
   - E.g., "Include annual training refresher and new-hire onboarding at no additional cost."

4. **Data & Customization:** Data migration included, reasonable customization allowance (e.g., 40 hours free custom development)
   - E.g., "Include historical data migration from [current system] and 40 hours of custom development at no additional cost."

5. **Contractual:** Favorable renewal terms (price cap for Year 2–3), flexibility to reduce licenses, termination clause
   - E.g., "Cap price increases at 3% per year for Years 2–3. Allow reduction of licenses with 60-day notice."

**Strategy:**
- Start with 3 finalists; leverage competition
- Reveal "leading vendor" to others: "Vendor B is currently leading. What can you do to close the gap?"
- Use reference feedback to challenge claims: "Your reference said implementation took 6 months, not 2 weeks. What's the typical timeline?"
- Set deadline: "We need final pricing by [Date]. Pending your response, we'll issue pilot proposal."
- Have walk-away point: "If you can't meet [requirement], we'll move forward with Vendor C."

**Deliverable: Vendor Negotiation Summary (DOCX)**
- Negotiation objectives (3–5 must-haves, 5–10 nice-to-haves)
- Talking points for each vendor (based on RFP responses and reference feedback)
- Financial targets (licensing discount target, implementation cost reduction target, etc.)
- Timeline for negotiation (when do we need final numbers?)
- BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement): Walk-away scenario and triggers

## Recommended Vendor Categories & Typical Competitor Sets

**Project Management Software** (Most Common Evaluation)
- Tier 1 (Mature, consolidated): Procore, Viewpoint (Vista), CMiC, Autodesk Build
- Tier 2 (Growing, niche): Touchplan (scheduling focus), Bridgit (crew optimization), Touchpages (financial controls)
- Typical evaluation: 4–5 vendors from Tier 1, 1–2 from Tier 2
- Common drivers: Multi-project controls, field productivity, real-time dashboards, financial integration

**Estimating Software**
- Tier 1: HCSS, B2W Software, Sage Estimating, ProEst
- Tier 2: PlanSwift (takeoff-first approach), McCormick Systems (parametric)
- Typical evaluation: 3–4 vendors
- Common drivers: Takeoff speed, historical database, labor rate management, integration with PM software

**Field Mobility / Mobile Apps**
- Tier 1: PlanGrid (now part of Autodesk), Fieldwire, Raken
- Tier 2: Procore mobile, JMP App, Trimble Field Hub
- Typical evaluation: 3–4 vendors
- Common drivers: Offline capability, crew time tracking, photo/document capture, integration with PM

**BIM / VDC (Virtual Design & Construction)**
- Tier 1 (Design Phase): Revit, ArchiCAD, Tekla Structures
- Tier 1 (Coordination): Navisworks, Solibri, BIM Collab
- Tier 1 (Scheduling): Synchro, Touchplan, Bridgit
- Tier 2 (Emerging): Buildots (document automation), ALICE (AI scheduling)
- Typical evaluation: 2–3 vendors per subcategory
- Common drivers: Clash detection, 4D/5D scheduling, progress tracking, AI automation

**Safety Software**
- Tier 1: Safety Culture, Salus Pro, Procore Safety
- Tier 2: iSAFE, Bridgit Bench (crew certification tracking)
- Typical evaluation: 2–3 vendors
- Common drivers: Incident tracking, corrective actions, OSHA integration, crew certifications, mobile reporting

**Financial / ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)**
- Tier 1: Sage 300, Viewpoint Vista, Foundation Software
- Tier 2: Procore Financial (integrated with PM), Buildots (document controls)
- Typical evaluation: 2–3 vendors (often integrated with PM evaluation)
- Common drivers: Job costing, accounts payable, payroll, financial reporting, multi-entity support

## Key Success Factors

**1. Requirements Clarity**
- Spend 2 weeks upfront on requirements. Vague requirements lead to wrong vendor choice and implementation failure.
- Get cross-functional alignment (operations, finance, IT, executive sponsor) on priorities and trade-offs.

**2. Honest Vendor Scoring**
- Score based on RFP responses and reference feedback, not on vendor's brand or pitch.
- If vendor doesn't answer a question, score it 1 (doesn't meet requirement), don't assume it's Yes.

**3. Reference Check Discipline**
- Call actual customers, preferably in similar firm size and project type.
- Don't accept vendor-suggested references without pushing for independent references.
- Challenge discrepancies between RFP claims and reference experience.

**4. TCO Transparency**
- Include all costs: licensing, implementation, training, support, integration, internal costs.
- Compare apples-to-apples (3-year and 5-year totals, not just license price).
- Use TCO to identify hidden cost risks.

**5. Negotiation Leverage**
- Articulate your BATNA (Best Alternative) early; vendors will ask.
- Use competition: "Vendor B is offering X; can you match?"
- Lock in multi-year terms for pricing/support stability.

**6. Executive Sponsorship**
- Secure executive buy-in on vendor recommendation before announcement.
- Brief losing vendors professionally; maintain relationships (they may be your next vendor).
- Plan change management for tool adoption from day 1.
