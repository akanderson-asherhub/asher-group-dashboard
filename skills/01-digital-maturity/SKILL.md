## DCCMM Framework: Six Dimensions × Six Maturity Levels

The **Digital Construction Capability Maturity Model (DCCMM)** is the scoring engine. Each dimension is scored 1–6, where 1 = Ad Hoc / Emerging and 6 = Optimized / Industry Leader.

### Six Dimensions

1. **Technology:** Hardware, software, cloud infrastructure, BIM/VDC capabilities, integration layers, cybersecurity posture, tech stack alignment with project delivery.

2. **People:** Skills inventory, training programs, digital literacy, organizational structure, change management readiness, recruitment/retention of tech talent, leadership buy-in.

3. **Process:** Workflow standardization, digital adoption roadmap, project delivery methodology integration, KPI tracking, continuous improvement mechanisms, documentation rigor.

4. **Strategy:** Digital vision alignment with business goals, innovation budget allocation, vendor roadmap, 3–5 year technology plan, competitive positioning, market differentiation.

5. **Digital Partnering:** Ecosystem relationships (consultants, technology vendors, subcontractors), collaborative tools adoption, information sharing maturity, supply chain digitization, partner integration capability.

6. **Environment:** Regulatory/compliance readiness, data governance, privacy/security frameworks, industry standards adoption (ISO, NIIST), client/owner integration requirements.

### Six Maturity Levels (Applied to Each Dimension)

- **Level 1 (Ad Hoc):** Manual, inconsistent, siloed. No formal process. Reactive approach.
- **Level 2 (Repeatable):** Some standardization. Process documented informally. Inconsistent execution.
- **Level 3 (Defined):** Formal process definitions. Documented. Applied across most projects.
- **Level 4 (Measured):** KPIs tracked. Data-driven process improvement. Consistent results.
- **Level 5 (Managed):** Continuous optimization. Proactive adjustments. Performance targets exceeded.
- **Level 6 (Optimized):** Industry-leading practice. Continuous innovation. Competitive advantage.

## Survey Intake Process

Aaron gathers the following from the client (via structured intake call + optional survey):

### Core Questions by Dimension

**Technology (8–10 questions)**
- Current tech stack inventory: PM software (which version?), BIM tools (Revit, Navisworks?), field tools (mobile), analytics platforms, CRM/financials integration
- Hardware: standardization, device refresh cycle, cloud infrastructure investment
- BIM/VDC maturity: % of projects in BIM, federated models, clash detection automation
- Integration: APIs, data silos, inter-tool workflows
- Cybersecurity: firewalls, access controls, incident response, client/partner integrations

**People (8–10 questions)**
- Org chart: dedicated digital/tech lead? BIM manager? IT resource?
- Training: formal programs? hours/year? onboarding rigor?
- Skills gaps: perception by leadership
- Recruitment: ability to attract/retain tech talent?
- Change management: resistance profile, leadership alignment

**Process (8–10 questions)**
- Project delivery methodology: Waterfall, IPD, Design-Build, Progressive Design-Build
- Digital adoption roadmap: exists? communicated?
- Standardization: templates, checklists, workflows documented?
- KPI tracking: project-level data, efficiency metrics, rework/quality metrics
- Continuous improvement: lessons learned process, feedback loops

**Strategy (6–8 questions)**
- Digital vision statement: explicit? aligned with CEO/COO vision?
- 3–5 year plan: documented? technology roadmap included?
- Innovation budget: % of revenue allocated to new tools/training?
- Vendor strategy: primary partners, long-term roadmap, replace/upgrade decisions
- Competitive positioning: how does digital differentiate in market?

**Digital Partnering (6–8 questions)**
- Key partners: consultants, tech vendors, subs, owners
- Collaboration tools: BIM 360, Slack, Microsoft Teams adoption
- Subcontractor/supplier digital requirements: enforced standards?
- Client/owner integration: do they push or pull digital adoption?
- Partner feedback loops: how are ecosystem issues addressed?

**Environment (6–8 questions)**
- Regulatory/compliance: GDPR, HIPAA, industry-specific requirements
- Data governance: data ownership, retention, access policies
- Client requirements: data location, security mandates, certifications
- Industry standards: ISO 19650 (BIM data management), NIIST compliance
- Risk/liability: insurance coverage for cyber, data breach contingency

**Supporting Questions**
- Revenue and project volume: context for engagement scope
- Geographic footprint: single market or multi-regional (affects standardization)
- Project types: heavy civil, commercial, residential (shapes tech priorities)
- Competitive landscape: how do peer firms rank digitally?
- Budget: what's the client willing to invest in digital transformation?

## Output Structure: 30–50 Page DOCX Assessment Report

### Section 1: Executive Summary (2–3 pages)

- **Headline finding:** Current maturity level across all dimensions (e.g., "Firm is at Level 3 (Defined) overall; strong in Technology, weak in Strategy and Digital Partnering")
- **Opportunity quantification:** Estimated ROI from addressing gaps (faster project delivery, cost savings, risk reduction, revenue uplift)
- **Quick-wins (next 90 days):** 3–5 low-cost, high-impact actions
- **Strategic priorities (12 months):** 3–5 investments to move from current to target maturity state
- **Roadmap preview:** Phased investment across dimensions and timeline

### Section 2: Current State Assessment (8–12 pages)

**Subsection 2.1: Dimension-by-Dimension Scores**

For each dimension:
- Current maturity level (1–6 with visual rating)
- Evidence from survey: quote key findings
- Benchmark comparison: "Peer firms in your segment average Level X; you are at Level Y"
- Top 3 strengths in this dimension
- Top 3 gaps in this dimension
- Industry benchmarks (e.g., "KPMG Canada/CCA notes that best-in-class GCs maintain BIM adoption on 80%+ of projects; you are at 45%")

**Subsection 2.2: Detailed Findings by Dimension**

- **Technology findings:** Current stack, integration gaps, modernization needs, cloud readiness
- **People findings:** Skill inventory, training investment, organizational structure, talent risk
- **Process findings:** Methodology adoption, standardization gaps, KPI tracking maturity
- **Strategy findings:** Digital vision clarity, innovation budget, vendor roadmap alignment
- **Digital Partnering findings:** Ecosystem strength, collaboration tool adoption, supply chain integration
- **Environment findings:** Compliance posture, data governance maturity, security readiness

### Section 3: Peer Benchmarking (2–3 pages)

- **Comparison table:** Current firm scores vs. peer benchmarks (industry average, top quartile, bottom quartile)
- **Peer definition:** "Peer firms = GC/ENR 400 with $500M–$2B revenue in commercial/mixed-use"
- **Visual:** Scatter plot showing firm vs. peers across dimensions
- **Interpretation:** "Firm leads in Technology but lags in Strategy — common pattern for fast-movers with underdeveloped roadmaps"
- **Reference benchmarks:** KPMG Canada/CCA construction-specific free tool, Autodesk AEC self-assessment, industry reports

### Section 4: Gap Analysis (3–4 pages)

**Subsection 4.1: Current vs. Target State**

For each dimension:
- Current maturity: Level X
- Target maturity (client-defined or industry benchmark): Level Y
- Gap: Y - X levels
- Estimated effort and investment to close gap
- Key dependencies and sequencing

**Subsection 4.2: Prioritized Gap Closure Roadmap**

- Phased approach: Phase 1 (0–3 months), Phase 2 (3–9 months), Phase 3 (9–18 months)
- By dimension, which gaps are quick wins vs. strategic investments
- Estimated budget per phase ($K)
- Expected capability lift per phase

### Section 5: Quick-Wins Heat Map (2–3 pages)

**Visual: 2×2 Matrix**
- X-axis: Effort (Low → High)
- Y-axis: Impact (Low → High)

**Quadrants:**

1. **Quick Wins (Low Effort, High Impact):** Implement immediately (0–90 days)
   - Examples: Mobile app rollout training, standardized template library, weekly digital huddles, process documentation

2. **Strategic Investments (High Effort, High Impact):** Phase into 6–12 month roadmap
   - Examples: BIM 360 implementation, ERP integration, culture change program, leadership training

3. **Low Priority (Low Effort, Low Impact):** Defer or combine with other initiatives
   - Examples: Secondary reporting, nice-to-have integrations

4. **Reconsider (High Effort, Low Impact):** Challenge or redesign
   - Examples: Tools with low adoption potential, redundant systems

### Section 6: Technology Recommendations (4–6 pages)

By dimension, recommend specific tools, frameworks, or practices:

- **Technology:** Upgrade priorities (BIM platform, PM software, analytics), integration roadmap, cybersecurity enhancements
- **People:** Training curriculum (BIM 101, mobile tools, data literacy), role definitions (BIM Manager, Digital Lead), recruitment targets
- **Process:** Methodology to formalize (e.g., IPD adoption), KPI dashboard recommendations, continuous improvement framework
- **Strategy:** Digital vision statement draft, 3–5 year tech roadmap outline, governance structure recommendation (Digital Steering Committee)
- **Digital Partnering:** Partner strategy (preferred consultants, key vendors), collaboration platform roadmap
- **Environment:** Compliance roadmap (ISO 19650?), data governance policies, security assessment follow-up

### Section 7: Implementation Roadmap (4–5 pages)

- **Phase 1 (Months 1–3):** Quick wins + foundational capability (e.g., process documentation, training launch)
- **Phase 2 (Months 4–9):** Strategic tech investments (tool implementations, organizational restructuring)
- **Phase 3 (Months 10–18):** Optimization and scaling (ecosystem integration, advanced analytics, competitive differentiation)
- **By phase:** Specific initiatives, responsible owners, success metrics, budget, dependencies
- **Governance:** Recommend a Digital Steering Committee (CFO/CTO + project leaders + ops) meeting quarterly

### Section 8: Investment Summary (1–2 pages)

**3-Year Budget Estimate (by dimension)**
- Technology: $XXX–$XXX (tools, hardware, cloud)
- People: $XX–$XX (training, hires, consultants)
- Process: $X–$X (documentation, process improvement)
- Strategy: $X–$X (planning, advisory)
- Digital Partnering: $X–$X (vendor management, integration)
- Environment: $X–$X (compliance, security)

**Expected ROI**
- Faster project delivery: X% reduction in project duration → $XXM annual savings
- Reduced rework: X% reduction → $XXM annual savings
- Improved safety: X% reduction in incidents → $XXM annual savings
- Revenue impact: Digital differentiation enables X% margin lift or X% premium on new work
- Payback period: X years

### Section 9: Closing and Next Steps (1 page)

- **Summary:** "Firm has strong foundational technology but needs strategic alignment and cross-functional governance. Investments in Phases 1–2 will unlock significant competitive advantage."
- **Immediate actions:** Convene Digital Steering Committee, validate target roadmap, kick off Phase 1 quick wins
- **Technology Roadmap engagement:** Invite Aaron to lead $25K–$75K Technology Roadmap engagement to detail Phases 2–3, vendor selections, and change management strategy

6. **Final validation:** Review Claude's scoring, add nuance, validate peer comparisons
7. **Closing narrative:** Craft compelling next-steps section that positions Aaron for $25K–$75K roadmap engagement
