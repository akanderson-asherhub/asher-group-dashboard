## Classification Framework: KPMG BuildingTech Taxonomy

All thesis work is organized around the KPMG BuildingTech taxonomy, which segments construction tech across the full asset lifecycle:

### 1. PropTech (Property Technology)
- Real estate and asset management solutions
- Sub-categories: Tenant/lease management, maintenance & operations, energy/sustainability, workplace/occupancy
- Key segments: Property management platforms, facilities management, building analytics
- Growth driver: ESG mandates, hybrid work, operational efficiency post-COVID

### 2. ConTech (Construction Technology)
- Construction operations and field management
- Sub-categories: Project management, field productivity, labor/safety, equipment management, supply chain, materials management
- Key segments: Job site software, workforce management, materials tracking, safety compliance
- Growth driver: Labor shortage, GC consolidation, data-driven optimization
- **Market Data:** 45% CAGR in ConTech funding; $11.2B PropTech capital in 2025

### 3. CRETech (Commercial Real Estate Technology)
- Analytics, transaction advisory, and decision-support
- Sub-categories: Market analysis, transaction advisory, valuation/appraisal, ESG/impact reporting, investment analysis
- Key segments: Real estate data/analytics, transaction platforms, portfolio analytics
- Growth driver: Institutional investor ESG requirements, data democratization

### 4. Smart Building Tech
- Building systems, IoT, and automation
- Sub-categories: Access control, occupancy analytics, HVAC/building automation, energy management, security
- Key segments: Integrated building controls, real-time occupancy/analytics, predictive maintenance
- Growth driver: Net-zero mandates, operational resilience, data-driven building management

## Critical Valuation Insight: SaaS Multiples Are Misleading

The investment thesis explicitly establishes why construction tech valuations must diverge from traditional SaaS multiples:

### The Problem: SaaS Math Doesn't Apply to Construction Tech
- **Enterprise SaaS Benchmark:** 10–20× ARR for growth-stage companies, with 80%+ gross margins, 5–10% net churn
- **Construction Tech Reality:**
  - Customer acquisition cost (CAC) is **2–3× higher** than vertical SaaS (sales-driven, field training required)
  - Annual churn is **20–30%**, vs. 5–10% for enterprise software (GCs change vendors, process incumbency breaks)
  - Sales cycle is **6–12 months**, not 3–6 months
  - Gross margin is 70–75%, not 80%+
  - **Result:** Construction tech trades at 5–8× ARR, not 12–15×, and requires longer CAC payback windows (18–36 months)

### Adjusted Valuation Framework for Construction Tech
**Magic Number Threshold:** For construction tech, sustainable 1.0× Magic Number (ARR Growth Rate / CAC Spend) is acceptable; >0.75× is healthy.

**Payback Period:** 18–36 month CAC payback is normal and should not be penalized if churn is <20% and TAM is large.

**Multiple Calculation:**
- Base: 5–8× ARR for Series A/B stage companies
- Adjustment for churn: -0.5–1.0× for every 5% above 15% annual churn
- Adjustment for CAC payback: -0.5× for payback >24 months (unless TAM >$2B)
- Adjustment for market position: +1–2× for category leader with durable competitive moat

**Example:** $5M ARR, 25% churn, 24-month CAC payback, clear #2 in category (emerging leader)
- Base: 6× = $30M
- Churn penalty: -0.5× = $27.5M
- CAC payback: no penalty (borderline acceptable)
- Market position: +1.0× = $37.5M–$40M
- **Fair valuation range: $35M–$45M (7–9× ARR)**

### Component 4: Valuation Methodology

**Objective:** Establish clear, disciplined valuation guidelines aligned to construction tech fundamentals (not SaaS comparables).

**Valuation Framework:**

**Stage-Based Pricing Model**

| Stage | Typical ARR | Valuation Method | Implied Multiple | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Seed/Pre-Revenue | $0–$100K | Milestone-based + founder equity stakes | N/A | Risk premium, team-dependent |
| Series A | $500K–$2M | 5–7× ARR + growth multiple | 5–7× | Conservative, given churn/CAC |
| Series B | $2M–$10M | 6–9× ARR, adjusted for metrics | 6–9× | Churn/CAC payback critical |
| Series C | $10M–$25M | 7–11× ARR, benchmark to comps | 7–11× | Market position & profitability matter |
| Growth/Late Stage | $25M+ARR | 8–12× ARR, comparable transactions | 8–12× | Rule of 40, path to profitability |

**Adjustment Factors (Applied to Base Multiple)**

1. **Churn Adjustment**
   - Baseline assumption: 15% annual churn
   - For every 5% above baseline: -0.5× multiple
   - For every 5% below baseline: +0.3× multiple
   - Example: 25% churn = -0.5× penalty; 10% churn = +0.3× premium

2. **CAC Payback Adjustment**
   - Baseline assumption: 20 month payback
   - <18 months: +0.2× (efficient sales)
   - 18–24 months: no adjustment
   - 24–30 months: -0.2× (sales-heavy model)
   - >30 months: -0.5× or investigate exit strategy

3. **Competitive Position Adjustment**
   - Category leader: +1.0–1.5×
   - Clear #2 or strong differentiation: +0.5–0.8×
   - Emerging competitor: no adjustment
   - Undifferentiated: -0.5–1.0×

4. **Market Growth Adjustment**
   - >30% annual growth: +0.5–0.8× (accelerated TAM expansion)
   - 15–30% growth: no adjustment
   - <15% growth: -0.3–0.5× (slower expansion)

5. **GC/Owner Concentration Risk**
   - Top 3 customers <30% revenue: no adjustment
   - Top 3 customers 30–50% revenue: -0.3× (concentration risk)
   - Top 3 customers >50% revenue: -0.8× (revenue at risk)

**Worked Example:**
- Company: $8M ARR, Series B
- Base multiple: 7.5× = $60M
- Churn 22% (-0.2×) = $54M
- CAC payback 22 months (no adj.) = $54M
- Clear #2 market position (+0.6×) = $62.4M
- Market growth 25% (no adj.) = $62.4M
- Top 3 customers 35% revenue (-0.2×) = $57.6M
- **Fair valuation: $57.6M–$62M range**

**KPMG "Nailing the Deal" Reference:** Emphasis on understanding that construction tech multiples reflect longer CAC payback and higher churn than traditional SaaS, and that TAM expansion (e.g., labor automation driving adoption) can offset churn over time.

### Component 6: Risk Management & Portfolio Mitigation

**Objective:** Identify systematic risks to construction tech investments and outline portfolio-level mitigation.

**Systematic Risks**

1. **Market Adoption Risk**
   - Construction is slow to digitize; GC procurement cycles are long
   - **Mitigation:** Build partnerships with Top 20 GCs early; target GCs with innovation programs; focus on high-pain-point applications (labor, safety)

2. **Churn Risk**
   - GCs switch vendors every 3–5 years; sticky software is rare in construction
   - **Mitigation:** Seek companies with strong retention metrics (<20% churn); build platform lock-in (data integration); bundle services

3. **Competition from Incumbent Consolidation**
   - Autodesk, Procore, Trimble acquire or build competing features
   - **Mitigation:** Partner with incumbents; avoid head-to-head on platform features; focus on niche or adjacent verticals

4. **Sales-Driven Model Economics**
   - High CAC + long sales cycles require patient capital
   - **Mitigation:** Seek companies with 18–24 month CAC payback; value profitability roadmaps; avoid pure growth-at-all-costs

5. **Founder Risk**
   - Domain knowledge required; construction tech founders are rare
   - **Mitigation:** Backup team capability; board-level operator engagement; retention incentives (earnouts, vesting cliffs)

6. **Integration Risk (Post-Acquisition)**
   - Roll-up thesis requires cultural, tech, and data integration; attrition spike post-close
   - **Mitigation:** Plan integration roadmap in diligence; retain key domain staff; preserve product autonomy where possible

**Portfolio-Level Mitigation**

| Risk | Fund-Level Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Churn concentration | Diversify across verticals (ConTech, PropTech, CRETech) and use cases (project mgmt, labor, operations) |
| Competitive consolidation | Partner with Autodesk, Procore ecosystem; build proprietary data assets; pursue adjacent segments |
| Macro construction cycles | Mix recession-proof use cases (safety, compliance, labor) with growth cases (digital transformation) |
| Stage risk | Balance Series A (high growth, high risk) with Series B/C (more mature, lower returns) |
| Geography risk | Expand internationally (Australia, EMEA) where digitization is earlier stage |

**Output:**
- 3–4 page Risk Management section
- Risk register template (XLSX with portfolio-level tracking)

- **CRETech:** 15–20% CAGR (data democratization, institutional investor demand)

**Key Tailwinds**
- Labor shortage in construction (40% of GC workforce retiring over next 10 years)
- Regulatory pressure (safety, ESG, building codes, labor compliance)
- Consolidation among GCs (favors integrated platforms, digital-native vendors)

**Key Headwinds**
- Legacy adoption cycles (GCs change slowly; average tool stack age 5–8 years)

- Churn (GCs experiment with tools but rarely commit long-term; competitive switching is cheap)
- Founder scarcity (hard to recruit construction industry operators with tech credibility)

**Geographic Opportunities**
- **Mature:** US (highest capital density, largest GCs, fastest digitization)
- **Growth:** Australia, EMEA, Canada (earlier stage digitization, GC consolidation emerging)
- **Emerging:** Latin America, Asia (future opportunity, limited capital currently)
