# Asher Group Engagement Statement of Work (SOW) Generator

## Engagement Type Selector & Pricing

Select the engagement type to customize SOW structure, deliverables, timeline, and fee.

### 1. Digital Maturity Assessment
- **Price Range:** $5,000–$10,000
- **Duration:** 4–6 weeks
- **Client Archetype:** GC/Owner, in-house assessment of current capability
- **Deliverables:** Maturity scorecard (5 dimensions, 3-point scale), gap analysis, priority roadmap
- **Typical Team:** 1 Principal, 1 Analyst

### 2. Tech Landscape Map (Quarterly)
- **Price Range:** $1,500–$5,000 per quarter
- **Duration:** 2 weeks per update
- **Client Archetype:** PE/CVC, Owner tracking sector trends
- **Deliverables:** Competitive positioning, emerging vendors, deal flow analysis, quarterly briefing
- **Typical Team:** 1 Principal

### 3. Tech Audit Sprint (Fast Diagnostic)
- **Price Range:** $20,000–$50,000
- **Duration:** 2 weeks (10 working days)
- **Client Archetype:** GC/Owner, quick diagnostic before larger engagement
- **Deliverables:** Current stack assessment, gap analysis, digital maturity scorecard, prioritized recommendations
- **Typical Team:** 1 Principal, 1 Analyst
- **vs. Market:** Boutique $40K–$100K; Big Four $75K–$150K

### 4. Vendor Evaluation Toolkit
- **Price Range:** $8,000–$15,000
- **Duration:** 3–4 weeks
- **Client Archetype:** GC/Owner evaluating 2–4 vendors for replacement
- **Deliverables:** Requirements matrix, vendor scorecard, financial analysis, negotiation playbook
- **Typical Team:** 1 Principal, 1 Analyst

### 5. Technology Roadmap
- **Price Range:** $25,000–$75,000
- **Duration:** 8–12 weeks
- **Client Archetype:** GC/Owner, strategic multiyear tech investment plan
- **Deliverables:** Current state assessment, market analysis, proposed roadmap, business case, implementation schedule
- **Typical Team:** 1 Principal (primary), 1 Analyst

### 6. Market Entry Strategy
- **Price Range:** $40,000–$100,000
- **Duration:** 10–14 weeks
- **Client Archetype:** GC vendor, pre-PMF product seeking market positioning
- **Deliverables:** Market sizing, competitive analysis, segment selection, GTM playbook, pricing framework, 12-month roadmap
- **Typical Team:** 1 Principal (primary), 1 Analyst

### 7. Business Case & ROI Analysis
- **Price Range:** $12,000–$25,000
- **Duration:** 4–6 weeks
- **Client Archetype:** Owner/GC, justifying technology investment to leadership
- **Deliverables:** Current process cost analysis, efficiency scenario modeling, financial model (3-year NPV/IRR), risk assessment
- **Typical Team:** 1 Principal, 1 Analyst

### 8. Commercial Due Diligence Brief
- **Price Range:** $10,000–$25,000
- **Duration:** 2–3 weeks per deal
- **Client Archetype:** PE/CVC investor screening construction tech acquisition
- **Deliverables:** Technology assessment, market validation, competitive positioning, team evaluation, financial model review, risk summary (20–35 pages + 2–3 page exec summary)
- **Typical Team:** 1 Principal, 1 Analyst

### 9. Investment Thesis Development
- **Price Range:** $25,000–$75,000
- **Duration:** 8–12 weeks
- **Client Archetype:** PE/CVC fund establishing construction tech thesis
- **Deliverables:** Sector positioning, sub-sector opportunity mapping, deal sourcing framework, valuation methodology, portfolio support model (30–50 page DOCX + PPTX IC deck)
- **Typical Team:** 1 Principal (primary), 1 Analyst
- **Fund Advisory Board Option:** $25K–$50K/year + potential carry

### 10. Advisory Retainer (Light)

- **Duration:** Ongoing, 6–12 month minimum
- **Client Archetype:** GC/Owner with episodic advisory needs
- **Deliverables:** 10 hours/month strategic advising, tech briefings, vendor introductions, steering committee attendance (as-needed)
- **Typical Team:** 1 Principal

### 11. Advisory Retainer (Active)
- **Price Range:** $15,000–$20,000/month
- **Duration:** Ongoing, 6–12 month minimum
- **Client Archetype:** PE/CVC or large GC with ongoing portfolio/strategic needs
- **Deliverables:** 20+ hours/month advising, sector intelligence, deal flow screening, steering committee attendance, monthly briefings
- **Typical Team:** 1 Principal, fractional Analyst
- **Fund Advisory Board Option:** $25K–$50K/year + potential carry

### 12. Fractional CINO (Chief Innovation Officer)
- **Price Range:** $15,000–$20,000/month

- **Client Archetype:** PE portfolio company or Owner-operator seeking operational tech leadership
- **Deliverables:** 30+ hours/month as fractional CINO: strategic guidance, vendor management, implementation oversight, board reporting
- **Typical Team:** 1 Principal

## 4-Phase SOW Structure (Standard Template)

### Phase 1: Discovery (Weeks 1–2)
**Objective:** Understand current state, stakeholder priorities, and constraints

**Key Activities:**
- Kickoff workshop with steering committee (2 hours)
- 8–15 stakeholder interviews (30–45 min each): operations, IT, finance, executive leadership
- Current data gathering: vendor list, financials, process documentation, org structure
- Competitive landscape snapshot (if applicable)

**Deliverable(s):**
- Discovery Report (5–10 pages): findings summary, top 3–5 themes, preliminary priorities, next-phase approach

**Client Responsibilities:**
- Sponsor steering committee kickoff attendance
- Timely interview scheduling (target: all interviews complete by end of Week 2)
- Provision of current systems documentation, org chart, budget baseline
- Identify and unlock internal data sources as needed

---

### Phase 2: Analysis & Design (Weeks 3–6)
**Objective:** Develop strategic options and validate direction

**Key Activities:**
- Market and competitive analysis (vendor landscape, benchmarks, case studies)
- Framework development (capability model, value drivers, decision criteria)
- Option generation: 2–4 strategic scenarios
- Steering committee validation workshop (2 hours)

**Deliverable(s):**
- Strategy Roadmap (15–25 pages): market context, option analysis, recommended pathway, phased timeline, resource estimate
- Steering committee sign-off on approach

**Client Responsibilities:**
- Steering committee participation in midpoint validation workshop
- Timely feedback on draft recommendations
- Internal alignment on strategic priorities (if divergence exists)

---

### Phase 3: Build & Align (Weeks 7–10)
**Objective:** Operationalize the strategy

**Key Activities:**
- Implementation planning: workstreams, milestones, resource allocation
- Business case development: financial model, ROI scenarios, risk/mitigation
- Vendor evaluation (if applicable): RFP support, scorecard, negotiation guidance
- Change management framework (organization, training, communication plan)

**Deliverable(s):**
- Implementation Plan (10–15 pages): phased roadmap, ownership, dependencies, key milestones
- Business Case Model: financial scenarios, sensitivity analysis, payback period
- Vendor shortlist + recommendation memo (if applicable)
- Change Management Summary: stakeholder plan, training outline, success metrics

**Client Responsibilities:**
- Assign implementation workstream leads
- Vendor selection decision (if applicable)
- Budget approval for implementation phase
- Identify change leadership and training resources

---

### Phase 4: Close-out (Weeks 11–12)
**Objective:** Finalize strategy and transition to execution

**Key Activities:**
- Final strategy document refinement and integration
- Risk register development and mitigation planning
- Transition plan and knowledge transfer
- Executive readout presentation to board/leadership

**Deliverable(s):**
- Final Strategy Document (40–60 pages): discovery findings, analysis, recommendations, implementation roadmap, risk register, appendices
- Risk Register: identified risks, probability/impact, mitigation owner, timeline
- Transition Plan: execution sequencing, vendor onboarding, training rollout, go-live checklist
- Executive Presentation (slides + speaker notes)

**Client Responsibilities:**
- Leadership attendance at executive readout
- Approve transition plan and risk register
- Establish post-engagement governance (steering committee continuity, if applicable)

## Payment Milestones (30/30/40 Structure)

- **30% at Kickoff:** Upon execution of SOW, upon workshop completion, and receipt of Phase 1 Discovery Report
- **30% upon Phase 2 Completion:** Upon Steering Committee sign-off on Strategy Roadmap and deliverables acceptance
- **40% at Final Delivery & Acceptance:** Upon delivery of Final Strategy Document, executive readout completion, and client sign-off on acceptance

All invoices due Net 15 from invoice date. Late payment subject to 1.5% monthly interest after 15 days.

## Negative Scope (Explicit Exclusions)

The following are explicitly OUT OF SCOPE and will be charged at the out-of-scope rate of $350–$500/hour if requested:

- Detailed software development or custom build specifications
- Implementation execution (staff augmentation during execution phase)
- Ongoing management or governance beyond transition period
- Board-level advisory (retainer engagements only, separate commercial terms)
- Direct vendor management or commercial negotiations on client's behalf
- Training delivery or end-user support
- Financial/tax/legal advice (referred to specialized counsel as needed)
- Market research or data subscription costs (passed through at cost + 10%)
- Travel and meals beyond agreed daily rate (actual expenses reimbursed)
- Any work requested without written Change Request (see Change Control section)

## Change Control

This SOW defines the Scope of Work. Any changes—additions, deletions, or modifications to deliverables, timeline, or approach—require a written Change Request approved by both parties BEFORE work begins on that item.

**Change Request Process:**
1. Client submits written description of requested change (email acceptable)
2. Asher Group provides revised estimate: impact on fee, timeline, and deliverables
3. Client approves estimate in writing
4. Work proceeds only upon approval; verbal requests will be acknowledged but not actioned without written confirmation

**No Unauthorized Scope Creep:** If additional work is requested mid-engagement without formal Change Request, the engagement is paused until Change Request is approved.

## Client Responsibilities & Dependencies

For successful engagement, Client commits to:

1. **Governance & Sponsorship**
   - Executive sponsor/steering committee chair assigned before kickoff
   - Steering committee available for scheduled workshops and sign-offs
   - Clear decision authority (who approves recommendations?)

2. **Access & Data**
   - Timely access to requested systems, documentation, and data
   - Provision of current org charts, process documentation, budget/cost baseline
   - Internal communications to stakeholders regarding interview requests

3. **Stakeholder Engagement**
   - Stakeholder availability for scheduled interviews (8–15 per Phase 1)
   - Prompt feedback on draft deliverables (5 business days target)
   - Leadership alignment on strategic priorities before Phase 2 completion

4. **Resources & Decisions**
   - Internal project manager or liaison to coordinate scheduling
   - Timely decisions on vendor selection, budget approval, implementation resourcing
   - Budget authorization for Phase 3/4 implementation activities (if included in scope)

5. **Commercial**
   - Timely payment per milestone schedule
   - No unauthorized scope expansion without Change Request

**If Client is unable to meet these responsibilities, the engagement timeline and deliverable quality may be materially impacted. Asher Group reserves the right to extend timeline or halt work pending Client action.**

## Out-of-Scope & Time & Materials Rate

Work outside the defined Scope (including change orders, expedited requests, and client-requested extensions) is billed at **$350–$500/hour** depending on complexity and urgency, in 0.5-hour increments.

Examples include:
- Additional analysis or modeling requested post-Phase 2
- Extended stakeholder engagement beyond original interview plan
- Client request for interim deliverables not in SOW
- Expedited review/revision cycles (rush premium: +20–40%)
- Post-completion consulting or implementation support

T&M hours are invoiced monthly with supporting detail and time tracking.

---

## Pricing Integrity Principle

**"If a client wants to negotiate price, adjust scope by removing deliverables rather than cutting the rate."**

Asher Group maintains pricing discipline to preserve analytical rigor and team quality. If the proposed fee is above Client budget:

1. Asher Group and Client jointly identify deliverables to remove (e.g., "eliminate vendor evaluation," "reduce interview count to 6," "provide summary-only roadmap")
2. Fee is reduced to match the reduced scope
3. A revised SOW documenting the reduced scope is executed

**Price floor:** $250/hour or $2,000/day minimum for any engagement.

## Intellectual Property & Confidentiality

### Ownership of Work Product
All work product delivered under this SOW—including reports, models, frameworks, recommendations, and analysis—is the property of Client, with a perpetual, non-exclusive license retained by Asher Group for:
- Anonymous case study reference and portfolio use
- Methodology refinement and improvement
- Training and internal knowledge base

Asher Group will not disclose Client name or identifying details without written permission.

### Confidentiality
All information provided by Client is treated as Confidential. Asher Group agrees not to disclose Client information to third parties without written consent, except:
- To subcontractors under written NDA
- As required by law or court order
- Anonymized in marketing/portfolio materials (with permission)

### Pre-Existing IP
Any tools, frameworks, or intellectual property developed by Asher Group prior to or outside this engagement remain Asher Group property. Client receives a license to use these as part of deliverables.

## Acceptance & Project Completion

Client acceptance of deliverables is deemed to occur upon:
1. Receipt of final deliverable(s)
2. No written objections received within 5 business days
3. Payment of final milestone (40%) invoice

If Client disputes deliverable quality within 5 days, Asher Group will meet to discuss and make reasonable revisions, provided the dispute is substantive and not a general dissatisfaction with recommendations. Approval of Phase 2 deliverables (Steering Committee sign-off) constitutes acceptance for purposes of Phase 2 payment milestone.
