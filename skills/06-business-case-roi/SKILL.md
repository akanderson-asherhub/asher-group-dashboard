# Business Case & ROI Model Development

## Engagement Context

**Fee Range:** Standalone $15K–$35K OR embedded in larger engagements (Technology Roadmap, Market Entry Strategy)
**Duration:** 2–3 weeks (standalone) or 1 week (embedded within larger engagement)
**Use Cases:**
  - Justify a specific technology investment (ERP, analytics platform, field mobility tool)
  - Support board presentation or investment committee approval
  - Secure financing for capital equipment (hardware, software licenses)
  - Evaluate M&A opportunity ("What's the ROI on acquiring this tech company?")
  - Baseline current-state costs before technology transformation
**Client Archetype:** GC operations teams, CFOs, CIOs evaluating technology spend; PE/VC investors conducting due diligence on construction tech targets

---

## Business Case Development Philosophy

A robust business case answers four critical questions:

1. **What will it cost?** (Total Cost of Ownership — TCO)
2. **What will we gain?** (Hard ROI + Soft ROI)
3. **Is the payback acceptable?** (NPV, IRR, payback period, ROI %)
4. **How sensitive is the case to my assumptions?** (What if adoption is 70% instead of 85%?)

This engagement delivers:
- **XLSX Financial Model** with detailed TCO, Benefits, and Sensitivity Analysis
- **DOCX Narrative** (10–15 pages) that makes the case to decision-makers
- **Executive-Ready Slides** (optional PowerPoint) for board or investor presentations

### Tab 2: Benefits & ROI Analysis

**Purpose:** Quantify financial benefits (hard ROI) and qualitative benefits (soft ROI) to justify the investment.

#### Hard ROI: Quantifiable, Dollar-Denominated Benefits

**Methodology:**
1. **Identify process:** What manual/inefficient process is the technology replacing?
2. **Baseline current cost:** Hours spent × loaded labor rate = annual cost
3. **Efficiency gain:** % reduction in hours (or cost) the technology provides
4. **Annual benefit:** Baseline cost × efficiency gain % = $X/year
5. **3-year cumulative benefit:** $X × 3 years (accounting for ramp-up in Year 1)

**Example 1: Labor Hour Savings (ERP Reducing Manual Journal Entries)**

*Current State:*
- 5 accounting staff × 10 hours/week on manual entries = 50 hours/week
- Loaded labor rate (salary + benefits + overhead): $75/hour
- Annual cost: 50 hours/week × 52 weeks × $75 = $195K/year

*Future State (Post-ERP):*
- Automation reduces manual entries 70%
- 5 staff × 10 hours × 30% remaining = 15 hours/week
- New cost: 15 hours/week × 52 weeks × $75 = $58.5K/year
- **Annual labor savings: $195K − $58.5K = $136.5K/year**

**Example 2: Rework Reduction (Field Mobility Tool Reducing Quality Escapes)**

*Current State:*
- 8% of work is reworked (construction industry baseline ~10%)
- Average project cost: $5M
- Rework cost: $5M × 8% = $400K per project
- Portfolio: 10 projects/year = $4M/year rework

*Future State (Post-Field Mobility):*
- Tool reduces rework to 5% (reduces quality escapes by 40%)
- New rework cost: $5M × 5% = $250K per project
- Portfolio: 10 projects/year = $2.5M/year rework
- **Annual rework reduction: $4M − $2.5M = $1.5M/year**

**Example 3: Schedule Compression (Advanced Scheduling Tool)**

*Current State:*
- Average project duration: 18 months
- Loaded cost of field overhead: $150K/month
- Project overhead cost: 18 months × $150K = $2.7M per project
- Portfolio: 5 projects/year = $13.5M/year

*Future State:*
- Scheduling tool compresses duration to 17.5 months (8% improvement via better resource scheduling)
- New duration: 17.5 months × $150K = $2.625M per project
- Portfolio: 5 projects/year = $13.125M/year
- **Annual overhead reduction: $13.5M − $13.125M = $375K/year**

#### Soft ROI: Non-Quantified or Difficult-to-Quantify Benefits

These benefits are real but harder to monetize. Document them for completeness and to strengthen the overall case.

**Typical Soft ROI Benefits in Construction:**

1. **Risk Reduction & Claims Avoidance**
   - Real-time data reduces disputes (photos, timestamps, compliance records)
   - Estimated: 10–20% reduction in claim frequency
   - Caveat: Hard to quantify exact dollar amount without claims history

2. **Improved Safety & Compliance**
   - Better incident tracking and near-miss identification
   - Reduced OSHA violations and associated fines
   - Lower workers' comp costs (see NSC benchmark below)

3. **Customer Retention & Satisfaction**
   - Better project visibility for owners
   - Faster RFI response times
   - Improved communication = higher NPS and repeat business

4. **Employee Retention & Morale**
   - Less manual paperwork = less field staff frustration
   - Improved tools = perceived investment in workforce
   - Reduced turnover (construction avg turnover 25–40%)

5. **Competitive Differentiation**
   - Technology becomes marketing advantage ("We use Procore, BIM 360, advanced analytics")
   - Ability to bid on larger, more complex projects
   - Potential for new service offerings (e.g., "data-driven project delivery")

6. **Strategic Data & Insights**
   - Historical project data becomes asset for estimating and bid strategy
   - Better cost intelligence across portfolio
   - Ability to identify underperforming processes and teams

#### ROI Metrics & Calculations

**Annual ROI % (After Year 1 Full Implementation):**

Formula:
```
Annual ROI % = [(Annual Benefits − Annual Ongoing Costs) ÷ Total Investment] × 100%
```

Using the example above:
- Annual hard benefits: $2,827,000 (from Tab 2, hard ROI row)
- Annual ongoing costs: $206,000 (Year 2 support costs, assuming implementation done)
- Total investment: $1,182,200 (Year 1 TCO, amortized)
- **Annual ROI % = [($2,827,000 − $206,000) ÷ $1,182,200] × 100% = 220%**

This is very strong — but likely optimistic. Apply sensitivity adjustments (see Tab 3).

**Payback Period (Months):**

Formula:
```
Payback Period = Total TCO ÷ (Annual Benefits − Annual Ongoing Costs) × 12 months
```

Using the example:
- Payback period = $1,182,200 ÷ [($2,827,000 − $206,000) ÷ 12] = ~5.5 months

A 5–6 month payback is excellent for construction technology. Most GCs target 12–18 month payback for capex projects.

**NPV (Net Present Value) at Discount Rates of 8%/12%/15%:**

Formula (simplified; standard NPV calculation):
```
NPV = Σ [Annual Cash Flow / (1 + Discount Rate)^Year] − Initial Investment
```

**Discount Rate Selection:**
- **8% Discount Rate:** GCs and stable contractors (low risk, predictable cash flows, strong balance sheets)
- **12% Discount Rate:** REIT/owner-developers (moderate risk, longer hold periods, regulated returns)
- **15% Discount Rate:** PE/CVC investors (higher risk, seeking returns above cost of capital, M&A targets)

**Example NPV Calculation (assuming $2.8M annual benefit, $206K ongoing cost, 8% discount rate):**

| Year | Cash Flow | Discount Factor (8%) | Present Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | ($1,182,200) | 1.000 | ($1,182,200) |
| 1 | $2,620,800 | 0.926 | $2,426,317 |
| 2 | $2,620,800 | 0.857 | $2,246,605 |
| 3 | $2,620,800 | 0.794 | $2,080,744 |
| 4 | $2,620,800 | 0.735 | $1,926,588 |
| 5 | $2,620,800 | 0.681 | $1,785,055 |
| **NPV @ 8%** | — | — | **$8,283,109** |

This $8.3M NPV over 5 years is compelling — every dollar invested returns ~$7 in net value.

**Recalculate at 12% and 15% discount rates for sensitivity analysis.**

**IRR (Internal Rate of Return):**

IRR is the discount rate at which NPV = 0. High IRR (>20%) signals strong investment.

Using financial functions (Excel IRR function or financial calculator):
- Plug in cash flows from above table
- Result: **IRR ≈ 180%** (very high, likely optimistic given benefit sensitivities)

**More conservative assumption (70% of estimated benefits):**
- Annual benefit: $2,827,000 × 70% = $1,979,000
- Annual cost: $206,000
- Net annual cash flow: $1,773,000
- NPV @ 12% (5-year): ~$4.2M
- IRR: ~120% (still strong, more defensible)

### Tab 3: Sensitivity Analysis

**Purpose:** Test how the business case holds up if assumptions change.

**Key Sensitivity Drivers (Ranked by Impact):**

1. **Adoption Rate** (±20%)
   - Impact: High (if only 65% of staff use tool vs. 85%, benefits drop 20%+)
   - Scenario: "What if we face user resistance and adoption lags?"

2. **Productivity Gain %** (±15%)
   - Impact: Very High (directly multiplies hard ROI)
   - Scenario: "What if the tool only delivers 50% of promised productivity vs. 70%?"

3. **Cost Reduction %** (±20%)
   - Impact: High (directly affects benefit quantification)
   - Scenario: "What if rework reduction is 25% instead of 37%?"

4. **Discount Rate** (±3 percentage points)
   - Impact: Medium (affects NPV, not payback)
   - Scenario: "What if our cost of capital rises to 15% instead of 12%?"

5. **Implementation Timeline** (±6 months)
   - Impact: Medium (delays cost realization)
   - Scenario: "What if implementation takes 8 months vs. 5 months?"

6. **Ongoing Support Costs** (±50%)
   - Impact: Low-to-Medium (ongoing costs grow but small relative to benefits)
   - Scenario: "What if vendor support is 50% more expensive than estimated?"

## Deliverable 2: DOCX Business Case Narrative (10–15 pages)

### Purpose & Structure

The DOCX document translates the XLSX analysis into a compelling, decision-maker-ready narrative. Tailor the tone and technical depth based on audience:
- **CFO/Finance Committee:** Emphasize payback period, NPV, and risk mitigation
- **Board/Executive:** Lead with strategic imperative and top-line ROI; bury complexity in appendices
- **Operational Staff:** Focus on process improvements and user benefits; make the financial case secondary

### Section 1: Executive Summary (1–2 pages)

**Opening Statement:**
Start with the business imperative, not the technology.

Example:
"Our company is losing $1.5M annually to rework and schedule delays that current systems cannot prevent. This business case justifies a $1.2M investment in [Technology X], which will reduce rework by 37%, compress project duration by 8%, and pay back in 6 months. We recommend immediate approval to begin implementation in Q2."

**Key Findings:**
- **Investment Required:** $1.18M over 3 years (or $X upfront capex)
- **Annual Benefit (Year 2 stabilized):** $2.8M (conservative: $2.0M at 70% adoption)
- **Payback Period:** 5–6 months
- **3-Year NPV @ 12%:** $3.85M
- **Recommendation:** APPROVE with phase-gate review at 6-month mark

**Audience-Specific Summary:**
If presenting to board, add:
- Strategic rationale: "Aligns with our 2026 strategy to become a technology-forward GC"
- Competitive positioning: "Our top 3 competitors (Turner, Skanska, McCarthy) have similar deployments; time to market is critical"
- Risk mitigation: "Implementation risk is LOW; we have 3 reference customers (names TBD) who are 6 months post-go-live with strong results"

## Key Takeaways for Scoping & Sales

1. **Anchor with Benchmarks:** Always reference McKinsey 14–15% productivity and 4–6% cost reduction. This signals industry expertise and gives the client external validation of assumptions.

2. **Payback Period is King for GCs:** Construction clients think in payback period (they want <12 months). Lead with payback period, not IRR or NPV.

3. **Soft ROI Matters:** While hard ROI drives the financial case, soft ROI (safety, retention, competitive positioning) drives emotional/strategic buy-in from executives.

4. **Sensitivity = Credibility:** Show conservative, base, and upside cases. This builds confidence that you're not cherry-picking optimistic assumptions.

5. **Always Include Risk Assessment:** What could go wrong? (Low adoption, extended timeline, lower-than-expected benefits.) How will you mitigate? Demonstrates mature thinking.

6. **Frame as Protection, Not Expense:** "This $1.2M investment protects us from losing $4M+ annually to rework and schedule delays." Frames technology as defensive spend, not discretionary.

7. **Follow-On Revenue:** Business cases often lead to Advisory Retainers (monitoring benefit realization) or Vendor Negotiation engagements (using ROI case to drive better pricing).
