## McKinsey 3-Phase Framework

This engagement uses McKinsey's "Decoding Digital Transformation in Construction" framework to structure the roadmap into three distinct phases with clear dependencies, cost profiles, and ROI expectations.

### Phase 1: Foundation (0–6 months)

**Objective:** Establish core digital infrastructure and operational baseline.

**Typical Initiatives:**
- Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) deployment or upgrade
- Core project management system implementation (portfolio, scheduling, resource)
- Financial systems integration
- Data governance foundation (master data, chart of accounts, WIP methodology)
- Business process documentation and standardization
- Organizational structure alignment

**Dependencies:** None — this phase has no predecessors.

**Initiative Table Structure (for XLSX tracker):**
Columns should include:
- Initiative Name
- Phase (Phase 1, 2, or 3)
- Priority (P1, P2, P3)
- Owner (department/function)
- Timeline (Start Q, End Q, Duration weeks)
- Cost Estimate ($K, capex vs. opex)
- Dependencies (previous initiatives)
- Success KPI (metric, baseline, target, measurement frequency)
- Current Maturity (1–5 scale)
- Target Maturity (1–5 scale)

**Example Phase 1 Initiatives:**

| Initiative | Priority | Owner | Timeline | Cost | Dependency | KPI |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ERP System Selection & Implementation | P1 | Operations | Q1–Q4 | $80K–$150K | None | Go-live date; % process coverage; training completion % |
| Accounting Integration | P1 | Finance | Q2–Q3 | $25K–$40K | ERP selection | Billing cycle time; AR/AP accuracy % |
| Scheduling System Rollout | P1 | Operations | Q2–Q3 | $15K–$30K | ERP selection | Schedule variance %; on-time delivery % |
| Data Governance Framework | P1 | IT/Operations | Q1–Q2 | $10K–$20K | None | Data quality score; process documentation % |
| Field Mobility Pilot (crew tablets) | P2 | Field Ops | Q3–Q4 | $20K–$35K | Core systems live | Daily report submission time; rework identification |

**Organizational Change Requirements (Phase 1):**
- Training: 40–60 hours per user (ERP, accounting, scheduling)
- FTE Impact: +1 to +2 FTE for program management, +0.5 to +1 FTE for training
- Change Management Milestones:
  - Month 1: Stakeholder alignment and kickoff
  - Month 2–3: Process mapping and system configuration
  - Month 4–5: UAT and training curriculum development
  - Month 6: Go-live and stabilization support

### Phase 2: Integration & Advanced Capabilities (6–18 months)

**Objective:** Leverage Phase 1 foundation to drive analytics, visibility, and field intelligence.

**Typical Initiatives:**
- Business Intelligence/Analytics platform (reporting, dashboards, predictive models)
- Building Information Modeling (BIM) integration with project management
- Field data capture automation (photogrammetry, mobile forms, IoT sensors)
- Advanced estimating tools (3D takeoff, historical database integration)
- Subcontractor collaboration portal
- Cost-to-complete and forecasting models
- Safety analytics and compliance dashboards

**Dependencies:** All Phase 2 initiatives depend on Phase 1 Foundation completion (ERP live, core processes stabilized).

- Training: 20–30 hours per user (analytics, BIM, field tools)
- FTE Impact: +1 to +1.5 FTE for analytics/data management, +0.5 FTE for process optimization

### Phase 3: Transformation Initiatives (18–36 months)

**Objective:** Deploy advanced technologies (AI, digital twins, predictive capabilities) that drive step-change performance and competitive differentiation.

**Typical Initiatives:**
- Artificial Intelligence and machine learning (risk prediction, resource optimization, document automation)
- Digital twin development (project representation for simulation and what-if analysis)
- Autonomous equipment management and predictive maintenance
- Labor productivity analytics (computer vision on jobsites, time tracking optimization)
- Supply chain visibility and optimization
- Sustainability tracking and embodied carbon reporting
- Integrated business planning (integrated forecast, capacity planning, scenario modeling)

**Dependencies:** All Phase 3 initiatives depend on Phase 2 Advanced Capabilities (analytics live, data infrastructure matured, organizational capability elevated).

- Training: 15–25 hours per user (AI literacy, digital twin usage, new workflows)

- Change Management Milestones:
  - Month 1–2: Pilot team selection, advanced training
  - Month 2–4: Pilot execution, results measurement
  - Month 4–6: Scale, refinement, organizational integration

## Technology Stack Recommendations by Category

Each category should include vendor options (leading + mid-market + point solutions) with sizing guidance based on client archetype (GC revenue, project count, headcount).

- **Category Focus:** GL, AP/AR, project accounting (WIP, job cost), revenue recognition

## ROI Benchmarks to Embed

All roadmap justifications must reference these McKinsey and industry benchmarks.

### Primary Benchmarks
- **McKinsey (Digital Transformation E&C):**
  - 14–15% productivity gains across labor-intensive operations
  - 4–6% cost reductions in project delivery
  - Full application across value chain: up to 45% project cost reduction

- **McKinsey Global Institute (Construction Labor Productivity):**
  - Construction labor productivity grew only 1% annually over 20 years vs. 2.8% for total economy
  - Opportunity: $1.6T in additional value creation if productivity matches broader economy

- **Deloitte/Autodesk (Technology Adoption):**
  - Each additional technology adopted = 1.14% increase in expected revenue across construction businesses

- **NSC (Safety Performance Correlation):**
  - Top-quartile safety performers: 20–40% productivity uplift and 15–20% insurance reductions

- **Aon (Risk Mispricing in Construction):**
  - Construction industry misprices risk by 20–40%, creating opportunity for improved risk management systems

### Calculation Methodology for Roadmap ROI

**Hard ROI Example (Phase 1 ERP + Scheduling):**
1. Baseline current cost: (field staff + office staff) × loaded labor rate × hours spent on manual processes = $X/year
2. Efficiency gain: ERP reduces journal entry time 60%, scheduling reduces rework 15%
3. Annual savings: ($X × 60%) + (rework cost × 15%) = $Y/year
4. 3-year payback: ($200K–$350K Phase 1 investment) ÷ ($Y annual benefit) = Z years

**Soft ROI (Phase 2 & 3):**
- Risk reduction: (current claim rate × average claim value) × 10–20% reduction = $X
- Compliance improvement: (audit finding remediation cost) × 30% reduction = $Y
- Retention improvement: (turnover cost per employee) × 5–10% reduction = $Z

**NPV Calculation:**
- Discount rates by client type:
  - GCs/stable contractors: 8% (lower risk, predictable cash flows)
  - REITs/owner-developers: 12% (moderate risk, longer hold periods)
  - PE/CVC investors: 15% (higher risk tolerance, exit-driven)
