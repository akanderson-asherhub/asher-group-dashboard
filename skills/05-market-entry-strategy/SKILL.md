## Deliverable 1: Market Opportunity Assessment (TAM/SAM/SOM)

### Strategic Objective
Size the addressable market using top-down (industry reports) and bottom-up (customer segment analysis) methodologies. Justify pricing, customer acquisition cost, and revenue model assumptions.

### Market Size Data (Use These Exact Numbers)

**PropTech Market:**
- **2025 Market Size:** $40B
- **2032 Market Projection:** $119B
- **CAGR:** ~17% through 2032
- **Source:** Leading industry reports (JLL, BCG, McKinsey construction tech analysis)

**ConTech Funding Market:**
- **2024 Funding Volume:** $4.8B
- **Funding Growth Rate:** 45% CAGR (2020–2024)
- **Mega-Rounds ($100M+) in 2025:** $11.2B deployed across ConTech
- **Funding Concentration:** Top 10 companies captured ~35–40% of 2024 funding

### TAM/SAM/SOM Structure

**TAM (Total Addressable Market):** $119B PropTech market by 2032

Break down by segment:
1. **Project Execution & Operations** (40% of TAM): Jobsite productivity, RFI/change management, field mobility, labor optimization
   - Key metrics: # of projects/year globally, avg project value, hours spent on manual processes
   - 2032 Estimate: $47.6B

2. **Financial & Commercial** (25% of TAM): Billing, payment, financial reporting, project accounting
   - Key metrics: # of GC/subcontractor companies, avg project revenue
   - 2032 Estimate: $29.75B

3. **Design, Estimation & Preconstruction** (20% of TAM): BIM, 3D takeoff, estimating, VDC
   - Key metrics: # of design/construction firms, annual design projects
   - 2032 Estimate: $23.8B

4. **Safety, Compliance & Workforce** (15% of TAM): Safety tracking, compliance, labor scheduling, training
   - Key metrics: # of construction workers, OSHA/compliance spending
   - 2032 Estimate: $17.85B

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market):** Apply geographic and customer segment filters

Example for a PropTech company with "field productivity" focus:
- **Geographic:** U.S. + Canada (assume 30% of global market) = $14.3B
- **Customer Focus:** GCs with $100M–$1B+ revenue (target 50% of market) = $7.15B
- **2032 SAM Estimate:** $7.15B

Example for a **ConTech startup** in the payments/financial space:
- **Geographic:** U.S., EU (assume 40% of global market) = $19B
- **Customer Focus:** Subcontractors, specialty contractors (assume 35% of market) = $6.65B
- **2032 SAM Estimate:** $6.65B

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):** Realistic capture within 5 years based on go-to-market strategy

For early-stage ConTech (Year 1 entry, typical Series A/B):
- Assume 2–5% market capture by Year 5 of SAM
- Example: $7.15B SAM × 3% = **$214.5M SOM** (Year 5 revenue potential)

For more mature company or later-stage:
- Assume 5–10% capture
- Example: $7.15B SAM × 7% = **$500M SOM** (Year 5 revenue potential)

### Revenue Model Assumptions

Structure the TAM/SAM/SOM analysis with explicit pricing and adoption assumptions:

**Assumption Set 1 (SaaS Subscription Model):**
- Price per user per month: $50–$500 (varies by solution category)
- Target user adoption curve: Year 1 (20% of TAM customers), Year 2 (35%), Year 3 (50%), Year 4 (65%), Year 5 (80%)
- Churn assumption: 5–10% annual (SaaS cohort model)
- ARR per customer: # users × price × 12 months × (1 – churn)

**Assumption Set 2 (Perpetual License + Services):**
- License fee: $100K–$500K per customer (one-time)
- Implementation/integration revenue: 50–100% of license fee
- Support & maintenance: 15–20% of license fee annually
- Customer lifetime value: (License fee + implementation) + (support revenue × 5 years)

**Assumption Set 3 (Marketplace/Transaction-Based Model):**
- Fee per transaction (supply chain match, payment processed, insurance premium): 2–5% of transaction value
- Average transaction size: varies by construction segment ($5K–$50K+)
- Monthly transaction volume growth: 20–30% YoY in early years, settling to 10–15% mature

### Sensitivity Analysis for TAM/SAM/SOM

Create a 3-scenario table for the executive summary:

| Scenario | Market Assumption | 2032 Market Size | SOM (Year 5 Capture) | Year 5 Revenue (@ pricing model) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Bear** | 12% CAGR (below PropTech trend) | $100B | 2% of SAM | $100–$150M |

## Deliverable 2: Technology Landscape Map

### Strategic Objective
Categorize competitive landscape by technology category, maturity stage, and customer segment. Identify white space and position client within landscape.

### Landscape Structure (3 Dimensions)

**Dimension 1: Lifecycle Phase** (where in the project is the solution deployed?)
1. **Preconstruction:** Design, planning, estimating, budgeting, approvals
2. **Procurement:** Supplier selection, RFQ/bidding, contract management, procurement
3. **Execution:** Jobsite management, field operations, labor/equipment tracking, quality
4. **Closeout:** Final accounting, warranty management, documentation
5. **Ongoing:** Post-project analysis, lessons learned, portfolio management

**Dimension 2: Technology Category** (what type of problem does it solve?)
1. **Collaboration & Communication:** RFI, change order, team messaging, document management
2. **Data & Analytics:** Dashboards, forecasting, predictive analytics, performance metrics
3. **Automation & Optimization:** AI/ML, workflow automation, resource optimization, scheduling
4. **Financial & Commercial:** Billing, payment, accounts payable/receivable, project accounting
5. **Physical Capture & IoT:** Photography/photogrammetry, sensors, drones, 3D scanning
6. **Marketplace & Ecosystem:** Supply chain matching, financing, insurance, workforce marketplace

**Dimension 3: Maturity Stage**
- **Emerging (Seed/Series A):** 0–3 years post-founding, <$5M ARR, early customer traction
- **Growth (Series B/C):** 3–6 years, $5M–$50M ARR, 20+ enterprise customers, proven product-market fit
- **Scale (Series D+/Post-Exit):** 6+ years, $50M+ ARR, 100+ enterprise customers, category leader or acquiree
- **Mature (Public/Private Equity):** Public or PE-backed, $100M+ ARR, profitability focus, market consolidation

## Deliverable 4: Go-to-Market Strategy

### Strategic Objective
Define customer segmentation, ICP, value proposition by segment, channel strategy, pricing, partnerships, and a phased 6-month launch roadmap.

### Customer Segmentation & ICP Definition

Develop 3–5 distinct customer segments, each with detailed ICP:

**Segment 1: Large GCs ($500M–$5B+ revenue)**
- **Archetype:** Turner Construction, Skanska USA, McCarthy Holdings
- **Key Traits:** Mature operations, complex projects, strong IT infrastructure, risk-averse
- **Problem Statement:** Need enterprise-scale platform to integrate 50+ regional offices; ROI must be proven and fast
- **Value Proposition:** 20–30% labor productivity uplift; integration with existing systems (Oracle, Viewpoint, SAP); 18-month payback
- **Buying Cycle:** 6–12 months (RFP, procurement, board approval)
- **Budget:** $500K–$2M first-year investment (software, implementation, training)
- **Decision-Maker:** VP Operations, CIO, CFO (group decision)
- **Objections:** "We're already using Procore." / "Implementation risk is high." / "Will this actually reduce headcount?"

**Segment 2: Mid-Size GCs ($50M–$500M revenue)**
- **Archetype:** Regional GCs, specialty contractors (mechanical, electrical, concrete)
- **Key Traits:** Growing operations, some degree of process maturity, tighter margin pressure
- **Problem Statement:** Procore costs too much ($50–$100/user/month); need field-first solution; tight budgets
- **Value Proposition:** 40% lower cost than Procore; faster implementation (4–8 weeks); offline field capabilities
- **Buying Cycle:** 2–4 months (direct sales, minimal procurement)
- **Budget:** $50K–$150K first year (software, training, internal setup)
- **Decision-Maker:** Operations Manager, Project Manager, sometimes CFO (depends on size)
- **Objections:** "Will I lose integrations?" / "Can I export my data?" / "What if you go out of business?"

**Segment 3: Subcontractors & Trade Partners ($2M–$50M revenue)**
- **Archetype:** Electricians, plumbers, concrete contractors, framing crews
- **Key Traits:** Often use spreadsheets or paper; very price-sensitive; mobile-first; high crew turnover
- **Problem Statement:** Manual paperwork takes 2+ hours/day; can't track crew productivity; constant RFI delays
- **Value Proposition:** Mobile app, offline capability, automatic photo logging, RFI response in hours not days
- **Buying Cycle:** 2–4 weeks (direct sales, low approval friction)
- **Budget:** $10K–$40K first year (per-crew pricing + mobile hardware)
- **Decision-Maker:** Project Manager or Foreman (hands-on)
- **Objections:** "I don't have time to learn new software." / "My crew doesn't use smartphones." / "What's the minimum project size?"

**Segment 4: Owners/Developers (including REITs)**
- **Archetype:** CBRE, Skanska Immobiliare, real estate development companies
- **Key Traits:** Long project timelines, regulatory complexity, multiple stakeholders
- **Problem Statement:** Can't easily monitor GC performance; lack real-time project visibility; regulatory compliance burden
- **Value Proposition:** Real-time dashboards for portfolio visibility; automated compliance reporting; risk analytics
- **Buying Cycle:** 3–6 months (contract reviews, procurement)
- **Budget:** $100K–$500K (site licenses for all projects + central analytics)
- **Decision-Maker:** Chief Development Officer, Project Controls Manager
- **Objections:** "Will GCs actually use this?" / "We need integration with our ERP." / "Is this confidential?"

**Segment 5: System Integrators & Software Partners (Reseller/Channel)**
- **Archetype:** Accenture, Deloitte, Bridgit, Procore ecosystem partners, regional tech integrators
- **Key Traits:** Sell solutions to construction clients; margin-focused; distribution reach
- **Problem Statement:** Gaps in their portfolio; need partner solution to complete offering
- **Value Proposition:** Reseller margin (20–35%); white-label option; integration support; co-marketing
- **Buying Cycle:** 1–3 months (partner agreement negotiation)
- **Budget:** $0 upfront (gain margin on customer sales)
- **Decision-Maker:** VP Partnerships, Business Development Manager
- **Objections:** "Does this compete with our other offerings?" / "Can I white-label it?" / "What's the minimum deal size?"

### Value Proposition by Segment

For each segment, define a 1–2 sentence value prop with quantified benefit:

**Large GC:** "Reduce labor costs 15–20% and achieve real-time visibility across 50+ project sites with enterprise-scale integration — paying 30% less than Procore."

**Mid-Size GC:** "Replace spreadsheets and Procore with field-first software that costs 50% less, implements in 6 weeks, and cuts RFI response time from 3 days to 6 hours."

**Subcontractors:** "Log 2+ hours of daily paperwork to a mobile app in 5 minutes, get automated photo RFIs, and stop losing money to rework and change orders."

**Owners:** "See every project, every day, in one real-time dashboard. Reduce claims 25%, catch schedule variance within 48 hours, not at month-end close."

**System Integrators:** "Add a field execution powerhouse to your portfolio. Earn 25–30% reseller margin. Co-go-market into your largest accounts."

**System Integrators:**

## Deliverable 5: Financial Projections & Business Case

### Pricing Model & Revenue Assumptions

Define the primary pricing model and unit economics:

**Assumption Set: SaaS Per-User Model (typical for field execution tools)**

- **Target Segments:** Large GCs (Segment 1), Mid-Size GCs (Segment 2)
- **Average Selling Price (ASP):** $75/user/month (blended across Tier A/B)
- **Target Average Customer:**
  - Large GC: 200 users, $18,000/month = $216K/year
  - Mid-Size GC: 50 users, $4,500/month = $54K/year
  - Blended ASP per customer: $135K/year

- **Implementation & Professional Services Revenue (one-time):**
  - Large GC: $100K–$200K
  - Mid-Size GC: $25K–$50K
  - Blended: $60K per customer (one-time)

- **Net Effective Revenue per Customer Year 1:** $135K + $60K = $195K (includes services)
- **Net Effective Revenue per Customer Year 2+:** $135K/year (recurring subscription only)

### Customer Acquisition & Growth Assumptions

Define your customer acquisition plan (reflected in sales spend):

**Assumptions:**
- **Year 1 Goal:** 8–12 customers (blended mix of Segments 1, 2, 3)
- **Year 2 Goal:** 25–35 customers (emphasis on Mid-Size GCs and Subs via integration partners)
- **Year 3 Goal:** 50–75 customers (market maturity, referral lift, integration partners bearing some acquisition)
- **Year 4 Goal:** 100–150 customers
- **Year 5 Goal:** 200–250 customers

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):**
- **Large GC (Segment 1):** $100K–$150K fully-loaded (senior sales rep time, travel, consulting)
  - CAC Payback: 7–12 months
- **Mid-Size GC (Segment 2):** $40K–$60K (standard sales rep, digital marketing mix)
  - CAC Payback: 6–8 months
- **Subcontractors (Segment 3, via partners):** $10K–$20K (partner-assisted, lower-touch)
  - CAC Payback: 9–18 months

### Unit Economics & Metrics to Track

**Quarterly Metrics Dashboard:**
- **MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue):** Recurring revenue ÷ 12
- **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue):** MRR × 12
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** Total sales & marketing spend ÷ new customers acquired
- **LTV (Lifetime Value):** ARPU × gross margin % ÷ churn rate
- **LTV:CAC Ratio:** Target 3:1 or better (each $1 spent on CAC yields $3+ lifetime value)
- **ARR per Customer:** Total ARR ÷ customer count (measures expansion and pricing power)
- **NRR (Net Revenue Retention):** (Beginning MRR + expansion + churn) ÷ beginning MRR × 100%
- **Customer Concentration:** % of ARR from top 10 customers (watch for >60% concentration risk)
- **Months to Profitability:** Cumulative positive operating cash flow

**Discount Rate Selection by Investor Type:**
- **GC/Strategic Buyer:** 10–12% (lower risk, strategic value)

### Sensitivity Analysis

Key drivers that most impact valuation:
- **Customer Acquisition:** ±20% changes customer count, drives ±25–30% valuation impact
- **Churn:** ±2% annual churn = ±30–40% impact on Year 5 ARR (compounding effect)
- **Pricing/ARPU:** ±10% = ±10% ARR impact (linear, unless affects customer mix)
- **Gross Margin:** ±5% = ±5–10% operating margin impact (material for profitability timeline)
- **Exit Multiple:** ±2x = ±20–25% valuation impact

## Client Archetypes & Customization

### Archetype 1: ConTech Startup Entering the Market
**Context:** Series A or B company ready to launch product in construction market; has product-market fit in adjacent sector (e.g., PropTech for commercial real estate) and is now targeting construction.

**Customization Emphasis:**
- Competitive positioning relative to established construction-only players
- SMB/mid-market segment focus (where margins are often higher than enterprise)
- Channel partnerships as primary growth lever (can't afford large direct sales team)
- Risk assessment: "How crowded is this niche?" (e.g., 50+ field execution tools exist)

### Archetype 2: GC Evaluating New Service Lines
**Context:** Established GC ($500M–$2B) exploring whether to launch a new offering (e.g., "we're a GC, should we be a developer too?" or "should we offer design-build?").

**Customization Emphasis:**
- Market sizing for the new service line within their current customer base
- Competitive analysis of other GCs doing this (who is winning, who is losing)
- Organizational capability assessment (do they have the talent?)
- Financial ROI specific to their operating model
- Partnership opportunities (joint ventures, acquisitions)

### Archetype 3: PE/CVC Investor Validating Investment Thesis
**Context:** Private equity or corporate VC firm considering investment in a ConTech company; wants to validate that the target market is real, the competitive positioning is defensible, and the financial projections are reasonable.

**Customization Emphasis:**
- Deep competitive analysis and win/loss testing (is the target company really winning against these competitors?)
- Customer reference calls with target customer list (do they actually want this product?)
- Comparable transaction analysis (what are similar ConTech exits valued at?)
- Risk factors (market saturation, customer consolidation, larger player competition)
- Portfolio complementarity (does this investment fit with other portfolio companies?)
